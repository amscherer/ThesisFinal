The Weight of Lead-Based Paint

The present threat of decades old paint: Using clothing to communicate data

Alix Marissa Scherer

Despite a nation wide ban that was enacted 45 years ago lead-based paint maintains a terrifying, often silent, unequal grip on the modern lives of Americans. While we may not be seeing advertisements for paint filled with lead, our City's children of today, and adults of tomorrow still face the lasting weight of health and developmental delays caused by chipped lead based paint.

The goal of this project is to explore the disproportionate harm of LBP on low- income New York City residents and present the findings in a wearable collection of clothing. In order to adequately represent the harm and prolonged health impacts of the exposure of LBP, each piece is designed using marbles in order to add weight to the experience of the data.
